What is water crisis?

Stimulate your mind as you test your typing speed with this standard A water crisis is a situation in which a region's available drinking, unpolluted water is lower than the demand of that region. Two converging phenomena drive water scarcity: the increasing use of freshwater and the depletion of usable freshwater resources. Life on Earth has nothing more essential than water and our ability to overcome water scarcity. Water is scarce, ranging from Central Australia to sub-Saharan Africa and the dense megacities of Asia. People struggle to access the clean water they need for their food to drink, cook, bathe, wash their hands, and grow.
